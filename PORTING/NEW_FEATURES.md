# Netplay with rollback (online multiplayer)

Found in: RetroArch, Dolphin Emulator
Lets you play multiplayer online with synchronization / rollback networking

👉 Why it’s cool:

Turns single-player retro systems into online multiplayer platforms
Dolphin even supports emulated LAN + online Wii features

➡️ Altirra:
No built-in seamless netplay ecosystem

# Run-ahead (near-zero input lag)

Found in: RetroArch, bsnes, Mesen
What it does: Removes 1–4 frames of latency by simulating future frames and rolling back.

👉 Why it’s cool:

Makes old games feel more responsive than original hardware
Huge for platformers and speedrunning

➡️ Altirra:
Focuses on accuracy → doesn’t aggressively “cheat time” like this

# Advanced shader pipelines (CRT simulation, etc.)

Found in: RetroArch, modern emulators
Multi-pass GPU shaders simulate:
CRT phosphors
scanlines
RF/composite artifacts
curvature & glow

👉 These can be extremely accurate visually

➡️ Altirra:
Has decent video filters, but not the massive shader ecosystems (1000+ shaders etc.)
